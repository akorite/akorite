Not every lantern longs for a window.

There are fires
that choose the cavern,
rivers that travel beneath stone,
and roots that drink in darkness
without once envying the leaf.

I have seen the dawn
spill its gold across the world,
yet even the sun, in mercy,
leaves half the earth in night.

For mystery is not deceit.

The pearl is hidden by the sea,
the seed by the loam,
the heart by the ribs of its own making.
Only the barren thing
demands to be entirely seen.

So keep a chamber unwitnessed.

Let there be a door
no census may open,
a path through the woods
that remembers no footsteps,
a silence that belongs
to no ear but your own.

For a soul stripped bare to every gaze
is a kingdom without walls,
a garden without shade,
a sky from which the stars
have been plucked and numbered.

And I would rather be

the moon behind a cloud,
the spring beneath the mountain,
the name carried away by the wind—

known only to those
I choose to call home.
